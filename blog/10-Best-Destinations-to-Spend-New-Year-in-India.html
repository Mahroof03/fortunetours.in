10 Best Destinations to Spend New Year in India

New Year celebrations begin much before the stroke of midnight on December 31. Christmas and the accompanying festive season is the time when everyone looks forward to pack their bags, and head to exotic destinations. If you are looking for the best New Year destinations in India, this list might help you.
 

Goa


 
Speaking of New Year and Christmas celebrations, Goa is the first place that comes to our mind. The atmosphere here livens up during the season. With midnight parties, music, and dance, Goa is where you let your hair down. Beachside parties and cruise parties sell like hot cakes during this season. Evening cruises with traditional Goan dances are intoxicating. A walk along the several Portuguese styled mansions is the best thing to do in Goa. The biggest advantage is the pleasant weather during New Year season.

Jaipur

The pink city is another awesome choice for spending a week in the most enjoyable manner. Festive lightings make the colorful city even brighter. Ancient palaces and magnificent architecture looks stunning against the backdrop of clear December sky. As the weather is cool and cozy, spending a night in a desert camp will be a good option. Live folk music and dance, combined with delicious Rajasthani food will be a different experience. Go for a camel ride through the desert, and you will love it.
 

New Delhi


 
When thinking of New Year celebrations, the capital city of India cannot be left behind. Delhi is the biggest crowd puller for winter holiday season. Though biting cold might make late night partying a bit difficult, there are enough indoor parties to keep the spirit alive. Piping hot and delicious street food in Delhi will be soothing in the cold of winter. Take small detours from Delhi, and you will find a lot of new experiences, ranging from colossal Mughal forts to rural life experiences.
 

Kolkata

Kolkata, better known as the city of joy, is where you make your celebrations happier. Nestled on the banks of Hooghly River, this city offers a multitude of attractions to visitors, whatever be the season. From colonial architecture to age-old temples, Kolkata has everything to seal a good deal for tourists in festive season. Roam around the city and taste its lip-smacking sweets, or head to Howrah Bridge for a stunning night view. Choice is yours.
 

Gulmarg


 
If you are a lover of white and fluffy snow, Gulmarg is one of the places where you can plan your winter holidays. This gem of Kashmir will be awfully cold in winters. But if you are willing to don a few extra woolens, there is no place like Gulmarg to enjoy the beauty of snow. From skiing to snowboarding, every winter sport is available during this period. But be prepared for sudden change of plans, in case the weather decides to be too rough.
 

Shimla

Shimla is another appealing winter destination where it will be chilly and cozy. There are chances that you will get to see a blanket of snow over the entire hill town. Mall Road and The Ridge are the most happening places in Shimla where you can witness New Year celebrations. Kufri near Shimla will come alive with winter sports such as skiing. Although Shimla is popularly known as a summer destination, spending winter holidays in this majestic hill town can be exciting too.
 

Kutch

If you are not very fond of chilling winter destinations, but wish to do something different for New Year celebrations, Kutch is an ideal option. Known for its vast expanse of white salt flats, Kutch is an amazing winter destination. The surreal beauty of Kutch is augmented in cool winter nights. RannUtsav, which is celebrated every winter, is a festival where you can enjoy music, dance, and local art forms of Kutch. Staying in tented accommodation on a full moon night will be a life-changing experience.
 

Andaman & Nicobar Islands

The islands of Andaman and Nicobar are an alternate solution for beach revelers who don’t prefer the crowd of Goan beaches. This normally laidback place puts on a festive look during the winter holiday seasons. There will be beachside parties, music, and cruises, and a lot more to do. Visit the happening places such as Havelock Island or Radhanagar beach, or get into a sunset cruise. Everything here will be thrilling experiences.

Kochi


 
Kochi, the Queen of Arabian Sea, is Kerala’s proud holiday destination. Winters are the best time to visit Kerala as the weather will be pleasant sans the biting cold. Kochi is melting pot of different cultures and cuisines. Visit the Jewish synagogue or watch a traditional Kerala art form, you are bound to be fascinated. It is also a place for trying out authentic Kerala sea food dishes. Fort Kochi is a place where you can watch Chinese fishing nets and colonial structures, and have a cup of coffee from quaint-looking roadside cafes.
 

Coorg

Coorg is better known as the coffee destination of Karnataka. But it is not just for coffee lovers. Everyone who loves to see the small bundles of natural beauty can head to this charming little hill station in Karnataka. Trekking, outdoor camping, and jeep safaris are popular activities in Coorg for New Year celebrations. There is an elephant camp too, where we can watch the tuskers in action.

Now there you have it! These are the best places in India to celebrate New Year 2020. So, which of these destinations are you planning to visit?
