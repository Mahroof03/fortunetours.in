Take a trip to the Largest Canal in Thailand: Saduak Floating Market

 

In the core of Bangkok near Ratchaburi province. A canal maze leads towards the ?Saduak Floating Market” - where foreigners row & collide their boats into the bustling side markets. 

  

This iconic market is a classic for anyone planning to explore Bangkok. Also widely seen in a popular James Bond film. But long ago…

    

Dating back to 100 years during the reign of King Rama V, the Saduak Floating Market was initially a place where local farmers and traders sold their goods to passing boats. Today, it has grown to become one of the most famous semi-aquatic attractions in Thailand, where people living near the canal come together to sell coconuts, t-shirts, decorations, tasty boat noodles and other food items you rarely find anywhere.


As you paddle through the market, you'll come across friendly locals who have been vendors, farmers, and fishermen here for generations. You'll be able to learn about their way of life and make new friends with a simple smile and a few kind words in Thai.

  

One of the highlights of the Saduak Floating Market is that vendors are eager to sell you everything from their boats. You can admire the traditional wooden houses on stilts and see the Saduak people's livelihood.

  

Make sure to head to the market after 9 a.m. which settles down the morning rush hour. Also bring between ?500 - ?800 to tour on a communal boat. One of the unique features of these traditional long-tailed boats is to navigate the narrow canals. These boats have a capacity of 5-6 people and have long propeller shafts, allowing the driver to steer & stop by markets with precision through the tight spaces. It's a thrilling experience to ride in one of these as you glide past other boats and vendors.


It’s common for vendors to charge foreigners a high amount, so have fun negotiating for a good deal. And never fall for the first price.


While the Saduak Floating Market is already an exciting place to visit, it truly comes to life during special events. One such event is the Loi Krathong festival in November. This is one of the peak rush seasons, where both locals and visitors come together to release beautiful lotus-shaped floats with candles and flowers into the canal. The sight of hundreds of glowing floats drifting down the canal is magical. Additionally, the festival offers traditional Thai music, dance performances, and food stalls selling delicious local delicacies. So, if you're planning a trip to the Saduak Floating Market, make sure to time it with one of these special events.


In conclusion, the Saduak Floating Market in Bangkok is an unforgettable travel experience. It's a journey worth paddling on your way to create memories lasting a lifetime. So what are you waiting for? Plan your trip today and explore this iconic market for yourself.
